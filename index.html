So what is scaling?

Scaling in video games refers to the process of adjusting various elements of a game to ensure a balanced and enjoyable experience for players.

This can involve scaling difficulty, graphical elements, performance, or content.
For this website in particular, we will be talking about DIFFICULTY scaling.

Scaling difficulty in games, refer to when the game changes difficulty based on a certain variable, such as the player level, count or something that the players do, such as an actual difficulty level they choose.

This is to make the game more enjoyable as people will be able to play the game while having a suitable amount of challenge.

Why IS it so important?

Difficulty scaling in video games is important to get right because unless YOU ARE A SOULS GAME
(ELDEN RING), terrible scaling will genuinely be a killer for your game due to it being TOO difficult or TOO easy.

A game being too difficult can be a strength of the game, however, if it's purely artificial difficulty such as increasing the damage and health of a basic enemy, IT IS NOT FUN. A game being too easy, unless it has an amazing story, is just BORING, due to there being no stakes or challenges.
